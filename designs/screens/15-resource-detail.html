What is an IEP?

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a legal document that outlines the specific educational services your child will receive. It is FREE and schools are required by law to provide it to eligible children with disabilities.

Your Key Rights
1
Request an IEP meeting in writing at any time
2
Have all documents translated to your language
3
Bring a support person or parent advocate
4
Disagree with the school's assessment in writing
