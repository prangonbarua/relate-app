# SECTION 1: IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) -- Deep Dive

## 1.1 Overview

The IDEA is the federal law that guarantees a **free appropriate public education (FAPE)** to eligible children with disabilities throughout the United States. Approximately **7.9 million children ages 3-21** received services under Part B of IDEA in the 2023-2024 school year. Autism is one of the **13 qualifying disability categories**.

**Legal Citation:** 20 U.S.C. Sections 1400-1482
**Key Regulations:** 34 CFR Part 300 (Part B); 34 CFR Part 303 (Part C)

Sources:
- [IDEA Overview - U.S. Department of Education](https://www.ed.gov/laws-and-policy/individuals-disabilities/idea)

## 1.2 IDEA的六大核心原则
|||九月|||
### 原则 1：零拒绝
- **任何儿童都不能被拒绝接受教育**，无论其残疾的性质或严重程度如何
- 接受联邦资助的学校必须无条件地教育所有残疾儿童
- 绝对适用于6-17岁；如果国家为 3-5 岁和 18-21 岁范围内的非残疾儿童提供服务，则可延长至这些年龄范围
- 学校不能因为认为孩子“太残疾而无法学习”而将其排除在外
- 学校不能因为孩子的残疾行为而将其开除
- 国会意图明确：“无条件、无例外”提供教育
|||九月|||
资料来源：【零拒绝-特殊教育简介】(https://viva.pressbooks.pub/intro2specialed/chapter/principle-1-zero-reject/)
|||九月|||
### 原则 2：子项查找
- 学校有**明确的法律义务**来识别、定位和评估其管辖范围内的所有残疾儿童
- 这适用于**出生至 21 岁**
- 涵盖公立学校、私立学校、家庭学校以及未就读任何学校的儿童
- 家庭**免费**进行评估
- 无论残疾严重程度如何均适用
- 学校必须制定积极的儿童查找政策和程序
- **对移民家庭至关重要：** 儿童查找适用于管辖范围内的所有儿童，无论移民身份如何
|||九月|||
资料来源：
- [儿童查找：学校有法律义务 - PAVE](https://wapave.org/child-find-schools-have-a-legal-duty-to-evaluate-children-impacted-by-disability/)
- [儿童查找：特殊教育法 - Euna Solutions](https://eunasolutions.com/resources/child-find-special-education-law/)
|||九月|||
### 原则 3：免费适当的公共教育 (FAPE)
- 每个符合条件的孩子都必须接受免费的公共教育**，家长无需付费**
- 教育必须“适当”——即根据每个孩子的需求量身定制
- **Endrew F. v. Douglas County (2017)** 提高了标准：IEP 必须“合理计算，以使孩子能够根据孩子的具体情况取得适当的进步”
- IEP 必须**“适当雄心勃勃”**——仅仅超过最低限度的进展是不够的
- “最低限度”（最低限度）标准被最高法院明确**拒绝**
- FAPE 包括特殊教育和相关服务
- FAPE 无论残疾类型、年龄或安置如何均适用
|||九月|||
**法律引用：** Endrew F. 诉道格拉斯县学区 RE-1, 137 S. Ct. 988 (2017)

## 1.2 Six Core Principles of IDEA

### Principle 1: Zero Reject
- **No child can be denied education** regardless of the nature or severity of their disability
- Schools receiving federal funding must educate ALL children with disabilities without conditions
- Applies absolutely between ages 6-17; extends to ages 3-5 and 18-21 if the state serves nondisabled children in those ranges
- A school CANNOT exclude a child because they believe the child is "too disabled to learn"
- A school CANNOT exclude a child for behavior caused by their disability
- Congressional intent is unequivocal: education is provided "unconditionally and without exception"

Source: [Zero Reject - Introduction to Special Education](https://viva.pressbooks.pub/intro2specialed/chapter/principle-1-zero-reject/)

### Principle 2: Child Find
- Schools have an **affirmative legal duty** to identify, locate, and evaluate ALL children with disabilities in their jurisdiction
- This applies from **birth through age 21**
- Covers children in public schools, private schools, home schools, and those not enrolled in any school
- Evaluations happen at **NO COST** to families
- Applies regardless of severity of disability
- Schools must have active policies and procedures for Child Find
- **Critical for immigrant families:** Child Find applies to ALL children in the jurisdiction regardless of immigration status

Sources:
- [Child Find: Schools Have a Legal Duty - PAVE](https://wapave.org/child-find-schools-have-a-legal-duty-to-evaluate-children-impacted-by-disability/)
- [Child Find: Special Education Law - Euna Solutions](https://eunasolutions.com/resources/child-find-special-education-law/)

### Principle 3: Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)
- Every eligible child must receive a free public education **at no cost to parents**
- Education must be "appropriate" -- meaning tailored to the individual child's needs
- **Endrew F. v. Douglas County (2017)** raised the standard: IEPs must be "reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances"
- IEPs must be **"appropriately ambitious"** -- merely more than minimal progress is NOT sufficient
- The "de minimis" (bare minimum) standard was explicitly **rejected** by the Supreme Court
- FAPE includes special education AND related services
- FAPE applies regardless of disability type, age, or placement

**Legal Citation:** Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District RE-1, 137 S. Ct. 988 (2017)

### 原则 4：最少限制环境 (LRE)
- 残疾儿童必须在适当的最大程度上与非残疾同龄人一起接受教育**
- 特殊教育是一种**服务，而不是一个地方**
- **通识课堂**永远是起点
- 仅当孩子即使有住宿、改造和补充援助也无法在普通教育中接受教育时，才从普通教育中除名
- **连续的安置**包括：
  1.有支持的通识教育课堂（助手、辅助技术、修改）
  2. 部分包含（普通版的部分内容，特别版的部分内容）
  3. 独立的特殊教育教室
  4. 专门学校
  5. 家庭指导
  6. 住宅设施
- 安置由 IEP 团队单独确定——没有一刀切的方法
|||九月|||

### 原则 5：程序保障
- 家长必须收到以其**母语**撰写的**权利书面解释**（程序保障通知）
- **事先书面通知 (PWN)：** 学校必须在更改孩子的身份、评估、安置或服务之前发出书面通知 - 必须解释他们的提议以及原因
- **家长同意书：** 在初步评估之前和首次提供特殊教育之前需要书面同意；父母可以随时撤回同意
- **语言要求：** 所有通知必须采用公众可以理解的语言以及家长的母语（包括盲文）
- 必须为失聪或母语不是英语的父母安排口译员
- 家长有权：检查记录、参加会议、独立评估、调解、正当程序听证会和国家投诉

### 原则 6：家长参与
- 家长是 IEP 团队的**平等成员**
- 家长有权参加所有有关孩子教育的会议
- 学校必须在双方商定的时间和地点安排会议
- 学校必须为英语水平有限的家长提供口译员
- 家长咨询和培训是 IDEA 下一项相关服务的法定权利（经常被学区忽视）

### Principle 4: Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)
- Children with disabilities must be educated **with nondisabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate**
- Special education is a **service, not a place**
- The **general education classroom** is always the starting point
- Removal from general education only when the child cannot be educated there even with accommodations, modifications, and supplementary aids
- The **continuum of placements** includes:
  1. General education classroom with support (aide, assistive technology, modifications)
  2. Partial inclusion (part of day in general ed, part in special ed)
  3. Self-contained special education classroom
  4. Specialized school
  5. Home instruction
  6. Residential facility
- Placement is determined individually by the IEP team -- there is no one-size-fits-all

Sources:
- [LRE in Special Education - Exceptional Lives](https://exceptionallives.org/blog/what-is-the-least-restrictive-environment-lre-in-special-education/)
- [LRE - Understood.org](https://www.understood.org/en/articles/least-restrictive-environment-lre-what-you-need-to-know)
- [LRE, Mainstreaming, Inclusion - Wrightslaw](https://www.wrightslaw.com/info/lre.index.htm)

### Principle 5: Procedural Safeguards
- Parents must receive a **written explanation of rights** (Procedural Safeguards Notice) in their **native language**
- **Prior Written Notice (PWN):** School must give written notice BEFORE changing a child's identification, evaluation, placement, or services -- must explain what they propose and why
- **Parental Consent:** Written consent required before initial evaluation and before providing special education for the first time; parents can withdraw consent at any time
- **Language Requirements:** All notices must be in language understandable to general public AND in the parent's native language (including Braille)
- Interpreters must be arranged for parents who are deaf or whose native language is not English
- Parents have rights to: inspect records, participate in meetings, independent evaluations, mediation, due process hearings, and state complaints

Sources:
- [10 Key Procedural Safeguards - Understood.org](https://www.understood.org/en/articles/10-key-procedural-safeguards-in-idea)
- [Procedural Safeguards - PAVE](https://wapave.org/procedural-safeguards-student-and-parent-rights-in-special-education/)
- [Procedural Safeguards - Wrightslaw](https://www.wrightslaw.com/info/safgd.index.htm)

### Principle 6: Parent Participation
- Parents are **equal members** of the IEP team
- Parents have the right to participate in ALL meetings about their child's education
- Schools must schedule meetings at mutually agreed-upon times and places
- Schools must provide interpreters for parents with limited English
- Parent counseling and training is a legal entitlement as a related service under IDEA (often overlooked by school districts)

Source: [Parent Counseling and Training - Autism Spectrum News](https://autismspectrumnews.org/the-best-kept-secret-school-districts-are-obligated-under-federal-law-to-offer-parent-counseling-and-training/)

# SECTION 2: THE IEP PROCESS -- Step-by-Step Guide for Parents

## 2.1 Step 1: Request an Evaluation

### How to Request:
1. Write a formal letter to the school principal or special education director
2. Date the letter and keep a copy for your records
3. Send via certified mail with return receipt (to prove delivery)
4. Include: child's full name, date of birth, grade, teacher's name
5. State clearly: "I am requesting a comprehensive evaluation for special education eligibility under IDEA"
6. Describe specific concerns about your child's learning, behavior, communication, or social skills
7. Attach any medical diagnoses (autism diagnosis), therapy reports, or outside evaluations
8. Request evaluation in ALL areas of suspected disability

## 2.2 Step 2: Evaluation Process

### What Evaluations Should Include for Autism:
- **Cognitive/IQ Testing** -- tailored to child's language abilities
- **Academic Achievement** -- current grade-level functioning
- **Speech-Language Assessment** -- expressive/receptive language, pragmatic language, administered by SLP
- **Occupational Therapy Assessment** -- fine motor, sensory processing, daily living skills
- **Psychological Assessment** -- social-emotional, behavioral, adaptive behavior
- **Autism Diagnostic Tools** -- ADOS-2 (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule), CARS-2
- **Adaptive Behavior** -- Vineland Adaptive Behavior Scales or similar
- **Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA)** -- if behavior is a concern
- **Motor Skills Assessment** -- gross and fine motor development
- **Sensory Processing** -- sensory profile, responses to stimuli

### Critical Requirements:
- Assessments must be in the child's **native language** or other mode of communication
- No single test can be used as sole criterion for eligibility
- Tests must be administered by trained professionals
- Assessment tools must be non-discriminatory (not culturally biased)
- Must assess in ALL areas of suspected disability
- Bilingual children must NOT be identified as having a disability solely because of limited English proficiency

## 2.5 了解 IEP 的目的和目标

**Before the Meeting:**
1. Review the current IEP carefully (highlight areas of concern)
2. Prepare a written "Parent Concerns" statement
3. List your top 3-4 priorities for your child
4. Gather supporting documents (therapy reports, progress data, work samples)
5. Send any private evaluations to the team at least one week before the meeting
6. Request copies of all school evaluations in advance
7. Consider inviting an advocate, therapist, or support person
8. Prepare questions in writing
9. If you need an interpreter, confirm arrangements in advance
10. Review notes from previous IEP meetings

**What to Bring:**
- Signed copy of your parent input/concerns letter
- Notebook for taking notes
- Audio recording device (check your state's rules; give 24-hour notice)
- Copy of current IEP (highlighted/annotated)
- Outside evaluation reports
- Work samples or videos showing your child's abilities
- List of questions
- A trusted person for support

**During the Meeting:**
- Ask for your concerns to be documented in the IEP
- Ask questions -- you do not have to agree to anything on the spot
- Take notes or record the meeting
- Request clarification of any jargon or acronyms
- Ensure every concern is addressed before the meeting ends
- You have the right to say "I need more time to think about this" before signing

**After the Meeting:**
- Review the final IEP document carefully before signing
- Signing means you ATTENDED, not necessarily that you AGREE
- You can write "I attended but do not agree with [specific section]"
- Request a copy of the finalized IEP
- Set up a system to track whether services are being delivered

## 2.7 Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA) and Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP)

### FBA:
- A systematic process for understanding WHY a behavior occurs
- Examines: Antecedent (trigger) --> Behavior (what happens) --> Consequence (what follows)
- **Required by IDEA** when a child is removed from placement for more than 10 days
- Should be conducted by a trained behavioral specialist
- Parents can request an FBA at any time

### BIP:
- A written plan developed from the FBA findings
- Outlines specific strategies for preventing and addressing challenging behavior
- Includes: proactive strategies, replacement behaviors, response procedures, data collection
- Must be part of the IEP when behavior impedes learning
- Should include positive behavioral supports -- NOT just punishment

## 2.8 Extended School Year (ESY) Services

### What is ESY?
- Educational services beyond the regular 180-day school year (typically summer)
- NOT the same as regular summer school -- individualized based on IEP goals

### Eligibility Criteria:
- **Regression/Recoupment:** Does the child lose skills during breaks? How long to relearn?
- **Severity of disability:** Autism is often considered a qualifying factor
- **Critical skill mastery:** Is the child at a breakthrough point?
- **Self-sufficiency:** Is the skill crucial for independence?
- If a child with autism loses communication, toileting, or social skills during breaks, this is a strong indicator
- ESY eligibility must be determined annually
- Every student with an IEP must be considered for ESY
- ESY services are free to parents

1. **与老师/团队交谈** -- 首先进行非正式讨论

## 2.10 How to Disagree with the School

### Options When You Disagree (escalating order):
1. **Talk to the teacher/team** -- Informal discussion first
2. **Request an IEP meeting** -- Put it in writing
3. **Write a formal disagreement letter** to the special education director
4. **Request an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)** at public expense
5. **File for mediation** -- free, voluntary, confidential
6. **File a state complaint** -- investigated by state education agency within 60 days
7. **Request a due process hearing** -- formal legal proceeding
8. **File an OCR complaint** -- for civil rights violations

### "Stay Put" (Pendency) Protection:
- During any dispute, the child remains in their current placement with current services
- Prevents the school from making changes while dispute is resolved
- Triggered when parents file for due process or mediation
- Remains active throughout any appeals process
- **One of the most important protections in special education law**

Source: [Stay Put Rights - Understood.org](https://www.understood.org/en/articles/stay-put-rights-what-they-are-and-how-they-work)

- 根据您不同意的学校评估，您有权享受一项公费 IEE
- 学校不能造成“无理延误”
- 您不必解释您不同意的原因
- 学校必须考虑 IEE 成绩

# SECTION 3: 504 PLANS

## 3.1 IEP vs. 504 Plan Comparison

| Feature | IEP (IDEA) | 504 Plan (Section 504) |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Law** | IDEA | Section 504, Rehabilitation Act |
| **Type** | Special education law | Civil rights law |
| **Eligibility** | 1 of 13 disability categories + adverse educational impact + need for specialized instruction | Any impairment substantially limiting a major life activity |
| **What it provides** | Specialized instruction + accommodations + related services | Accommodations only |
| **Written document** | Detailed IEP with goals, services, placement | Plan listing accommodations (less formal) |
| **Review schedule** | Annual (required) | Periodically (typically every 3 years) |
| **Age coverage** | Ages 3-21 | All ages through college |
| **Due process rights** | Extensive (IDEA protections) | Less extensive |

## 3.2 504 与 IEP 何时合适
- **504：** 儿童有限制主要生活活动的残疾，但不需要专门指导
- **IEP：** 孩子需要专门指导、修改课程和/或相关服务
- **对于大多数自闭症儿童：** IEP 提供更强有力的保护和更多服务
- 在某些情况下，孩子可以两者兼有

- 安静的测试环境
- 感官工具（坐立不安、降噪耳机）
- 社交技能支持
- 日程变更提前通知
- 不知所措时允许离开课堂

## 4.1 Informal Negotiation Strategies
1. Document every concern in writing (email creates a paper trail)
2. Request an IEP meeting to discuss disagreements
3. Bring an advocate or support person to meetings
4. Keep your tone professional and focused on the child's needs

## 4.2 Mediation
- Free to parents (paid by the state)
- Voluntary -- both parties must agree
- Confidential -- cannot be used in later proceedings
- Facilitated by a trained, impartial mediator
- Agreements are legally binding
- Cannot delay a parent's right to a due process hearing

## 4.3 Due Process Hearing
- Formal legal proceeding before an impartial hearing officer
- Parents can represent themselves or hire an attorney
- School must convene a "resolution meeting" within 15 days of receiving complaint
- 30-day resolution period before hearing proceeds
- Statute of limitations: Generally 2 years

## 4.4 国家投诉
- 向国家教育机构备案
- 指控学校违反 IDEA 要求
- 必须在 60 天内调查并解决

# SECTION 5: SPECIFIC RIGHTS FOR IMMIGRANT FAMILIES IN SCHOOLS

## 5.1 Plyler 诉 Doe (1982)——基金会
|||九月|||
**所有儿童都享有免费公共 K-12 教育的宪法权利，无论移民身份如何。**
|||九月|||
- 美国最高法院裁决（1982 年 6 月 15 日）
- 违反平等保护条款，因移民身份而拒绝接受教育
- 国家不能扣留无证学生的教育资金
- 各州不能根据孩子或父母的移民身份拒绝入学
- 这适用于所有公立学校服务，包括特殊教育
|||九月|||
**法律引用：** Plyler 诉 Doe，457 U.S. 202 (1982)
|||九月|||
资料来源：

## 5.2 学校不能询问移民身份
- 学校不能要求学生提供社会安全号码来注册
- 学校不能要求公民身份或移民身份证明
- 学校不能要求“绿卡”、签证或护照
- 学校不能拒绝外国出生证明的入学
- 学校不能向任何机构报告移民身份
- 2011 年美国能源部/司法部致亲爱同事的信强化了这些保护措施
|||九月|||
资料来源：[移民学生权利 - Colorin Colorado](https://www.colorincolorado.org/immigration/guide/rights)
|||九月|||
## 5.3 FERPA 保护
- 《家庭教育权利和隐私法》保护所有学生记录
- 未经家长书面同意或有效的司法令状（由法官签署，而非 ICE 行政令状），学校不得与 ICE 或移民机构分享学生信息
- 学校不得收集或共享移民身份信息
- 家长有权审查并要求更正学生记录

**ALL children have the constitutional right to a free public K-12 education, regardless of immigration status.**

- U.S. Supreme Court ruling (June 15, 1982)
- Violation of the Equal Protection Clause to deny education based on immigration status
- States cannot withhold funding for education of undocumented students
- States cannot deny enrollment based on immigration status of the child OR parents
- This applies to ALL public school services, including special education

**Legal Citation:** Plyler v. Doe, 457 U.S. 202 (1982)

Sources:
- [Plyler v. Doe - American Immigration Council](https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/fact-sheet/plyler-v-doe-public-education-immigrant-students/)

## 5.2 Schools CANNOT Ask About Immigration Status
- Schools cannot require Social Security numbers for enrollment
- Schools cannot require proof of citizenship or immigration status
- Schools cannot require a "green card," visa, or passport
- Schools cannot deny enrollment for a foreign birth certificate
- Schools cannot report immigration status to any agency
- The 2011 Dear Colleague Letter from DOE/DOJ reinforced these protections

Source: [Immigrant Students' Rights - Colorin Colorado](https://www.colorincolorado.org/immigration/guide/rights)

## 5.3 FERPA Protections
- Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act protects all student records
- Schools CANNOT share student information with ICE or immigration agencies without written parent consent OR a valid judicial warrant (signed by a judge -- NOT an administrative ICE warrant)
- Schools must not collect or share immigration status information
- Parents have the right to review and request corrections to student records

## 5.4 当前的移民执法和学校（2025-2026）
|||九月|||
**重要更新：**
- 自 2025 年 1 月起，国土安全部废除了“敏感地点”政策

  - 只有**司法令**（来自法官）需要遵守——而不是来自 ICE 的行政令
  - 学校可以通过行政令合法拒绝访问

## 5.5 Right to Language Access
- Interpreters for ALL school meetings, conferences, and communications -- free
- Translated documents of important school information -- free
- Schools CANNOT use students, siblings, or untrained staff as interpreters
- Schools must translate important documents into the most common languages
- If information is not in your language, you can request a translated copy
- These rights apply to IEP meetings, parent-teacher conferences, discipline hearings, and ALL communications

## 5.6 Title III: Language Instruction for English Learners
- Federal program for supplemental services for English Learners
- EL status does NOT disqualify a child from special education
- Not speaking English is NOT a disability
- EL status alone cannot be the basis for a special education referral
- Conversely, being an EL cannot be used to DENY a special education evaluation

Source: [Title III - Virginia DOE](https://www.doe.virginia.gov/programs-services/federal-programs/essa/title-iii)

## 5.7 双语特殊教育注意事项
- 评估必须使用孩子的母语或沟通方式
- 儿童不得仅仅因为英语水平有限而被认定为残疾
- IEP 目标应纳入考虑英语水平的语言目标
- 研究表明患有自闭症的双语儿童可以用两种语言学习
- 母语教学不会阻碍读写能力的发展
- 患有自闭症的 DLL/EL 患语言困难的风险并不比单语儿童更大
- IEP 团队应包括英语学习者和第二语言习得方面的专业知识

### TEACCH Method:

- Parents should be trained in TEACCH strategies for home consistency

- Social stories
- Structured teaching
- Positive behavior support
- Peer-mediated instruction
- Video modeling

- Transition Meeting held at least 90 days (no more than 9 months) before child's 3rd birthday

# SECTION 9: POST-SECONDARY TRANSITION

## 9.1 Transition Services Under IDEA (Age 16+)
- IEP must include transition goals no later than age 16 (some states require 14)
- Focused on: post-secondary education, vocational education, employment, adult services, independent living
- Student MUST be invited to participate in IEP meetings about transition
- Goals based on age-appropriate transition assessments
- Key areas: Education/Training, Employment, Independent Living

Source: [Transition to Adulthood - CPIR](https://www.parentcenterhub.org/transitionadult/)

## 9.2 Vocational Training and Pre-Employment Services
- **Vocational Rehabilitation (VR):** Career counseling, job training, job search help, workplace accommodations, job coaching
- **Pre-Employment Transition Services (Pre-ETS):** Job exploration, work-based learning, job readiness, post-secondary counseling, self-advocacy

## 9.3 College Disability Services
- ADA mandates accessible higher education
- IEPs do NOT transfer to college
- Standard changes from FAPE to "equal access"
- Students must self-advocate and register with disability services

## 9.4 超龄后的成人服务
- IDEA 服务于毕业或 21-22 岁结束
- 不会自动过渡到成人服务 - 家庭必须主动计划
- 重点计划：医疗补助豁免、职业康复、SSI/SSDI、州 DD 服务、支持性就业、住宅服务
|||九月|||
## 9.5 ABLE 账户
- 为残疾人士提供税收优惠储蓄账户
- 资格：26岁之前出现残疾；接收 SSI 或 SSDI
- 资金不影响公共福利的资格
- 可用于：教育、住房、交通、医疗保健、辅助技术、职业培训
- “教育”的定义很广泛——包括证书课程、成人教育、专业培训
|||九月|||

- 学校说“让我们拭目以待”而不是评估
- 评估不全面（缺失区域）
- 评估不是用孩子的母语进行的
- 学校声称英语水平有限解释了所有困难
|||九月|||
### IEP 文件问题：
- 当前级别部分含糊、笼统或有错误的名称/代词（复制粘贴）
- 没有列出家长关心的问题
- 目标不可衡量或每年都相同但没有进展
- 尽管有记录的需求，但相关服务仍缺失
- 尽管存在行为挑战，但没有 BIP
- 尽管需要沟通，但没有考虑辅助技术

## 9.5 ABLE Accounts
- Tax-advantaged savings accounts for individuals with disabilities
- Eligibility: disability onset before age 26; receiving SSI or SSDI
- Funds do NOT affect eligibility for public benefits
- Can be used for: education, housing, transportation, healthcare, assistive technology, job training
- "Education" is broadly defined -- includes certificate courses, adult education, professional training

Source: [ABLE FAQ - ABLE National Resource Center](https://www.ablenrc.org/frequently-asked-questions/)

# 第 13 节：权利快速参考
|||九月|||
- 您的孩子有权接受**免费**公共教育——您无需支付任何费用
- 您的孩子有这项权利**无论移民身份如何**（Plyler v. Doe，1982）
- 学校里没有人可以询问您的移民身份
- 您有权在所有学校会议上获得**口译员** - 免费
- 您有权免费获得**翻译文件**
- 您有权**请求特殊教育评估**——免费
- 学校**必须**在法定期限内回复您的评估请求
- 所有测试必须以您孩子的**母语**进行
- 您是 IEP 团队的**平等成员**
- 您可以**带任何人**参加 IEP 会议以获得支持
- 您可以**不同意**学校的意见并要求更改
- 您可以请求进行**独立评估**，费用由学校承担
- 您的孩子不能因残疾造成的行为而被开除
- 未经**您的同意**（或正当程序），学校不得更改服务
- 如果学校侵犯您孩子的权利，您可以提出**投诉**
- 可通过每个州的**家长培训中心**获得免费帮助
- FERPA 保护您孩子的学校记录不被与移民机构共享

- **Set up:** ABLE account if eligible
- **Age 18:** Student may assume own educational rights
- **Graduation or age 21-22:** IDEA services end

# SECTION 13: RIGHTS QUICK-REFERENCE

- Your child has a right to a **free** public education -- you pay nothing
- Your child has this right **regardless of immigration status** (Plyler v. Doe, 1982)
- No one at school can ask about your immigration status
- You have a right to an **interpreter** at all school meetings -- free
- You have a right to **translated documents** -- free
- You have a right to **request an evaluation** for special education -- free
- The school **must** respond to your evaluation request within the legal timeline
- All testing must be in your child's **native language**
- You are an **equal member** of the IEP team
- You can **bring anyone** to IEP meetings for support
- You can **disagree** with the school and request changes
- You can request an **independent evaluation** at the school's expense
- Your child cannot be expelled for behavior caused by their disability
- The school cannot change services without **your consent** (or due process)
- You can file a **complaint** if the school violates your child's rights
- Free help is available through **Parent Training Centers** in every state
- FERPA protects your child's school records from being shared with immigration agencies
