# Relate
### *A free, multilingual app that trains immigrant parents to become their autistic child's daily therapist.*

> "You know your child best. We help you help them."

---

## The One-Line Pitch

Relate fills the 27-month gap between autism diagnosis and real care — by training immigrant parents, in their language, using real ABA techniques, built around their specific child.

---

## The Problem

### Three crises happening at the same time

**1. The Wait Crisis**
- 1 in 31 US children have autism — and growing
- Average wait from first concern to actual therapy: **27 months**
- 61% of clinics have 4+ month waitlists. 15% are closed to new patients entirely
- The US needs **5x more ABA therapists** than currently exist
- During that 27-month wait: zero guidance, zero support, zero tools

**2. The Immigrant Crisis**
- Black, Asian, and Hispanic children have *higher* autism rates than White children — but receive *far less* care
- Only 10% of California pediatricians offered autism screening in Spanish
- Translated diagnostic tools "miss the cultural mark" — wrong cutoffs, altered meanings
- Bangladesh: **3% autism rate** in Dhaka — among the highest globally — with almost zero services
- India: **1 in 65 children** — 1.8 million kids — mass underdiagnosis
- East African immigrant children: **3.5x more likely** to have autism with intellectual disability
- One 2025 research title says it plainly: *"As immigrants, we are all lost in our autism journey"*

**3. The Girls Crisis**
- Real male:female autism ratio is 3:1 — but diagnosis rates show 4–5:1
- Girls **mask** autism — they mimic social behavior, appear fine, and get diagnosed years later
- The global standard diagnostic tool excludes autistic females at **2.5x the rate** of males
- In immigrant families: a quiet, compliant girl is praised — her autism goes unnoticed until adolescence

## The Core Insight

> A therapist visits 10 hours a week.
> A parent is there **112 hours a week**.
> The parent is the real therapist. We just need to train them.

- **90%** of children improve significantly when caregivers are involved in therapy hours
- Parent-implemented ABA = **47.7% reduction** in behavioral issues vs. general parent education alone
- The therapist unlocks the door. **The parent keeps it open every day.**

## App Features

### 1. Pre-Diagnosis Track — "Does This Look Familiar?"
Most apps require a diagnosis to do anything. Relate starts before that.

- Pre-screening checklist in 12+ languages
- "Here's what you noticed. Here's what it might mean. Here's what to do next."
- Videos narrated by immigrant parents from the same community — not doctors
- Cultural reframing: *"It is not a curse. It is not your fault. It is not shameful."*
- Explains what the school legally owes your child under IDEA — even before a diagnosis

**Phase 1 Languages:** Bengali, Hindi, Urdu, Tamil, Spanish, Somali
**Phase 2 Languages:** Amharic, Tagalog, Vietnamese, Arabic, Haitian Creole, Portuguese, Sinhala, Nepali

---

### 2. Child Profile Builder — "What Do You Know About Your Kid?"
The parent fills in:
- What their child loves (foods, textures, sounds, routines)
- What triggers them (transitions, loud sounds, crowds, eye contact)
- How they currently communicate (words / pointing / sounds / nothing)
- What they're already good at
- Family language and cultural background

Every daily card, every suggestion, every activity is built around this profile. It gets smarter as the parent logs more over time.

---

### 3. The 5-Minute Daily Card
**The heart of the product.**

Every morning, one card. One skill. Five minutes.

```
TODAY'S SKILL: Teaching "I need a break"

WHY IT MATTERS
When your child can't say they're overwhelmed,
they melt down. This gives them a signal.

WHAT TO DO (5 minutes)
During dinner, when things are calm:
1. Show them a "break card" (picture of quiet corner)
2. Say: "When you need quiet, show me this"
3. Practice twice — calm setting only

WHAT TO LOOK FOR
Did they look at the card? Grab it? That's progress.

[ I tried it ]  [ Not today ]  [ I have a question ]
```

- Personalized to the specific child's profile
- Rotates through: communication, self-care, routines, social awareness, sensory regulation
- 5 minutes — a parent working two jobs can do 5 minutes
- Microlearning format: **17% better retention**, **50% more engagement** than long-form content

---

### 4. Self-Care School
**Teaching kids independence — through parents.**

For older autistic children, the biggest gap is daily living skills. These cannot be taught in a clinic. They happen at home.

Each module:
1. Why this matters — 1-min narrated video
2. The steps, broken down visually — parent and child view together
3. Sensory alternatives — "if your child can't handle scented soap, here are 3 fragrance-free options under $5"
4. How to practice it — embedded into morning routine
5. Progress tracker — parent logs what happened

**Skills Library:**
- Brushing teeth / showering / deodorant / shaving
- Managing periods (sensory-safe product options)
- Getting dressed (sensory-friendly clothing options)
- Making a simple meal
- Recognizing overwhelm before meltdown
- Safe touch and body autonomy
- Social scripts: "how do I say hi," "how do I ask for help"
- Managing school, public transport, public spaces

---

### 5. Insurance + Rights Navigator
**What families are owed — and how to actually get it.**

Most immigrant parents don't know:
- Their child has federal rights under IDEA regardless of immigration status
- Schools must provide free therapy by law
- Insurance denials can be appealed — and usually win when done correctly
- Medicaid waivers exist and how to get on the list

**Built-in tools:**
- State-by-state insurance coverage breakdown
- Insurance appeal letter template — fill in name, diagnosis, denial date, send
- IEP meeting scripts — exactly what to say when the school says they can't do more
- Local resource directory filtered by: language spoken, insurance accepted, current waitlist status

---

### 6. BCBA Async Layer
**Expert guidance without telehealth.**

Telehealth fails this population:
- No reliable internet in rural/low-income areas
- Parents can't schedule around shift work
- Language barriers make live calls impossible
- Cultural discomfort with video calls about sensitive topics

**What we do instead:**
- Parent logs observations and short activity videos
- Partnered BCBA records a 3-minute video response — no scheduling, anytime
- Parent watches on the bus, during lunch — whenever
- BCBA adjusts the child's program quarterly

One BCBA can serve **20 families** this way in the time a live session covers one.

**The staffing model:**
- **Lead BCBA** — designs program, reviews logs, sets goals
- **Parent** — daily implementer, trained through cards and modules
- **AI (Claude)** — fills every gap between human touchpoints

---

### 7. Community — "Parents Like Me"
- Matched by: child's age, diagnosis level, language, country of origin, location
- Moderated by BCBAs who answer questions weekly
- Parents share what's working in their own language
- Peer testimony from someone who looks like you is more trusted than anything clinical

---

### 8. Crisis Mode
Child is melting down right now.

Parent opens app → **3 steps immediately** based on their child's profile.
No reading. No navigating. Just: do this, then this, then this.
